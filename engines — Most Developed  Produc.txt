### **Tier 1 — Most Developed / Production-Ready**

1. **Andrew Hill Engine**
   Interval-cell driven composition with asymmetric phrasing and harmonic ambiguity; includes full generator, validator, and export pipeline.

2. **Wayne Shorter Engine**
   Motif-first, form-aware modal engine with ambiguity handling; includes runtime generator, validator, MusicXML export, and UI/launcher workflow.

3. **Songwriting Engine (Compiler / Song IR System)**
   Fully re-architected “compiler-style” system using Song IR, hook-first generation, identity scoring, and section compilers; strongest system-level design.

4. **Guitar–Bass Duo Engine (LOCK + DCR Integrated)**
   High-precision duo composer enforcing counterpoint, bass-as-co-composer, motivic integrity, and strict GCE validation with MusicXML output.

---

### **Tier 2 — Strong but Not Fully Unified**

5. **Jimmy Wyble Counterpoint Engine**
   Two-line polyphonic guitar system focusing on independence, contrary motion, and idiomatic dyads; musically strong, narrower scope.

6. **Ellington Orchestration Engine**
   Big band orchestration logic engine for section colour, density control, and voicing strategies; strong concept, less runtime integration.

7. **Big Band Architecture Engine**
   Structural planner for large ensemble form (intro, shout, solos, backgrounds); excellent macro-design, lacks full generation pipeline.

---

### **Tier 3 — Emerging / Partially Implemented**

8. **Conductor Engine (Concept / Prototype Stage)**
   Meta-engine coordinating: form → harmony → counterpoint → orchestration → export across engines; not fully implemented yet.

9. **Polyphonic Labyrinth Engine**
   Barry Harris–influenced polyphonic system with density layers and diminished pivots; conceptually rich, not yet fully built.

10. **Triad Pair / Jordan Klemons Integration Layer**
    Harmonic colour system using triad pairs for melodic generation; partially embedded but not formalised as a standalone engine.

---

### **Tier 4 — Modifiers / System-Wide Rules (Critical but Non-Standalone Engines)**

11. **LOCK System (Global Generation Protocol)**
    Hard constraint framework enforcing DCR, GCE ≥9, chord-melody alignment, bass integrity, and export validation.

12. **DCR Protocol (Design → Commit → Refine)**
    Internal iteration system ensuring high-quality single-pass outputs (5–7 internal drafts before commit).

13. **Grand Criteria of Excellence (GCE) Engine**
    Evaluation framework for musical quality across melody, harmony, rhythm, interaction, and form.

14. **DTE v2.1 (Double Time Excellence Framework)**
    Specialized evaluator for perceived velocity, harmonic motion, and counterpoint clarity in double-time contexts.

15. **Bass Harmonic Integrity Gate**
    Ensures bass lines imply harmony correctly (root/3rd/7th presence, voice-leading, no harmonic ambiguity failures).

16. **Melody Lock + Chord Alignment Gate**
    Prevents harmonic clashes with melody; enforces legality of tensions and structural coherence.

17. **Rhythm Anti-Loop Rule**
    Prevents repetitive rhythmic cells beyond two bars unless intentionally varied.

18. **Section Contrast Gate**
    Forces A/B/C sections to differ in density, register, and rhythmic identity.

19. **Export Validation Gate**
    Ensures MusicXML structural correctness (measures, parts, parsing integrity).

---

### **Tier 5 — Conceptual / Directional Extensions**

20. **“Mighty Ten Engines” Expansion Concept**
    Framework for adding composers (Messiaen, Stravinsky, etc.) into a unified engine ecosystem.

21. **Generative Music Primitives System**
    Underlying architecture of motif, harmony, rhythm, phrase, orchestration modules; partially distributed across engines.

22. **Song Identity / DNA Scoring System**
    Evaluates uniqueness, hook strength, and memorability; strongest in songwriting engine but not global yet.

## **Reality Check (Important Insight)**

* The **most advanced “thing” is not an engine** → it’s the **system layer (LOCK + DCR + GCE)**
* The **best musical outputs currently come from Guitar–Bass + Hill/Shorter hybrids**
* The **biggest gap is orchestration integration** (Conductor Engine not finished)
* The **next leap is NOT another engine** → it’s **unifying everything under Conductor + Primitives**

---

If you start V2.0, the hierarchy should flip to:

**System (Conductor + Primitives) → Engines → Modifiers → Export**

Right now it’s the reverse, which is why things are slowing down.
