# Anti-Monotony Rules

**Version 1.0**  
**Universal — applies to all styles, ensembles, projects**

---

## Anti-Repetition Rule (Mandatory)

**No musical cell longer than 4 bars may repeat more than twice without transformation.**

---

## Valid Transformations

When a cell has repeated twice, apply one or more of:

- **Register shift** — move material to different octave
- **Interval expansion** — widen or narrow intervals
- **Rhythmic displacement** — shift attack points
- **Orchestration change** — reassign to different instrument(s)
- **Harmonic reharmonization** — new chord progression under same melody
- **Counterline introduction** — add new voice
- **Texture change** — density, timbre, or role shift
- **Density shift** — thicken or thin

---

## Application

- **4-bar cell:** Max 2 identical repetitions (8 bars total) before transformation.
- **2-bar cell:** Max 2 identical repetitions (4 bars total) before transformation.
- **1-bar cell:** Max 2 identical repetitions (2 bars total) before transformation.

---

## Texture Rule

- No texture longer than 8 bars unchanged.
- Introduce variation in texture, rhythm, or harmony within 8 bars.

---

## Reject Conditions

- Cell repeated 3+ times identically
- Static texture > 8 bars
- No motivic development across sections
