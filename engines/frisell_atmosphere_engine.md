# Frisell Atmosphere Engine

## Harmonic Language

Floating tonal centres; suspended chords (sus4, sus2); pedal points; slow harmonic drift. Use Tonality Vault, modal palettes. Avoid dense clusters; prefer open voicings.

## Rhythmic Philosophy

Spacious and suspended. Rubato-feel within notated discipline. Phrase spillover across barlines. Delayed arrivals. Slow harmonic rhythm.

## Orchestration Behavior

*Adapt to any ensemble:* Primary melodic (lyrical fragments, sustained lines); secondary (independent, responsive counterpoint); inner (transparent, inner motion); bass (pedal tones). At least 30% reduced texture; silence is compositional.

## Motif Development Approach

Lyrical fragments developed through register shift, texture change, harmonic reharmonization. Gradual transformation rather than abrupt contrast. Counterline introduction in selected sections.

## Structural Tendencies

Gradual bloom or dissolution. Texture evolution over time. Static fields with internal motion. Density arc: low → development → peak → release.
