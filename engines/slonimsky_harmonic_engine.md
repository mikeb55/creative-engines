# Slonimsky Harmonic Engine

## Harmonic Language

Symmetric scales and modes; interval cycles; exotic chord voicings; non-diatonic colour. Use interval cycles, Tonality Vault. Avoid conventional diatonic clichés.

## Rhythmic Philosophy

May combine with any rhythmic engine. Angular melodic phrasing. Interval-driven rhythmic emphasis. Episodic development.

## Orchestration Behavior

*Adapt to any ensemble:* Primary (Slonimsky-derived intervals, angular lines); secondary (mirror or invert intervals); inner (exotic colour); bass (symmetric movement). Intervallic content must be perceptible.

## Motif Development Approach

Interval expansion and contraction. Symmetric formal designs. Motifs developed through inversion, transposition, interval cycle. Systematic rather than random.

## Structural Tendencies

Episodic development. Interval expansion/contraction. Symmetric formal designs possible. Density arc: low → development → peak → release.
