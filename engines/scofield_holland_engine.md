# Scofield–Holland Groove Engine

## Harmonic Language

Modern modal jazz. Triad pairs, upper-structure voicings. Use Tonality Vault, triad pairs, or polychords. Avoid long monophonic passages; prefer dyads, triads, chord fragments in harmonic-capable instruments.

## Rhythmic Philosophy

Syncopated attacks; short melodic fragments; rhythmic echoes between voices. Groove-based, not rubato. Medium tempo (100–110 bpm). Rhythmically forward-moving, not floating.

## Orchestration Behavior

*Adapt to any ensemble:* Primary melodic voice (responses, fragments); secondary (rhythmic echoes, harmonic colour); inner voices (counterpoint engine); bass voice (harmonic anchor, drives groove). At least one voice must function as bass anchor.

## Motif Development Approach

Short cells developed through groove variation. Rhythmic displacement; orchestration change. Avoid literal repetition; transform through syncopation and dialogue.

## Structural Tendencies

Clear sectional architecture. Development through groove variation. Ensemble hits as punctuation. Bass anchors each section. Density arc: low → development → peak → release.
