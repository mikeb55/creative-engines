# Shorter Narrative Engine

## Harmonic Language

Modal interchange; chromatic pivot chords; non-functional harmonic motion; upper-structure harmony. Use Tonality Vault, interval cycles, polychords. Avoid static tonal loops, II–V cycling, predictable progressions.

## Rhythmic Philosophy

Asymmetrical phrase lengths (5, 7, 9 bars). Delayed arrivals and resolutions. Motif-driven rhythmic displacement. Breath and release. Melody leads harmony.

## Orchestration Behavior

*Adapt to any ensemble:* Primary melodic voice (motif development, narrative arc); secondary (counterlines, dialogue); inner (harmonic grounding); bass (narrative, non-functional). At least one section must feature dialogue between two voices.

## Motif Development Approach

Start with small motif (2–5 notes). Develop via interval expansion, rhythmic displacement, inversion, harmonic reinterpretation. Each section transforms the motif rather than repeats it. Harmony shifts narrative direction at least twice.

## Structural Tendencies

A (motif introduction) — B (transformation) — C (harmonic expansion) — A' (reinterpreted return). Clear sectional architecture. Density arc: low → development → peak → release.
