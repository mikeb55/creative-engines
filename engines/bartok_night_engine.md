# Bartók Night Engine

## Harmonic Language

Modal folk scales; static harmonic fields; sudden harmonic shifts; dissonance for colour. Use Tonality Vault, modal palettes, polychords. Avoid predictable functional harmony.

## Rhythmic Philosophy

Irregular phrase lengths; folk-inspired patterns; rubato in selected passages; sudden rhythmic interruptions. Static fields with internal motion.

## Orchestration Behavior

*Adapt to any ensemble:* Primary (folk-inspired melody); secondary (texture, tremolo, sustained, percussive); inner (drones, nature-like effects); bass (pedal, drone). At least one section uses texture (tremolo, mute, ponticello) for nocturnal character.

## Motif Development Approach

Folk motifs developed through register shift, texture change, harmonic reharmonization. Sudden outburst as contrast. Transformation in return (A').

## Structural Tendencies

A (mysterious intro, static field) — B (folk melody or outburst) — A' (return transformed) — Coda (dissolution or sudden close). Density arc with sudden spike at outburst.
