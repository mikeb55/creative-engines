# Wheeler Lyric Engine

Models Kenny Wheeler–like lyrical modern jazz: long-arc melody, suspended harmony, wide intervals used lyrically, chamber-jazz atmosphere, and asymmetric phrase design.

## What This Engine Does

The Wheeler Lyric Engine generates:

- **Long-arc melodic writing** — Sustained phrases, not fragmented
- **Emotional interval contour** — 4ths, 5ths, 6ths, 9th-like leaps
- **Suspended harmony** — maj7, sus4, sus2, spacious and warm
- **Wide intervals used lyrically** — Singable, not angular
- **Chamber-jazz / ECM atmosphere** — Floating, emotionally coherent
- **Asymmetric phrase groups** — 5–7, 7–9, 9–11 bars
- **Transformed returns** — Emotional lift on reprise

## How It Differs From Shorter / Bartok / Hill

- **vs Shorter**: Less angular, more sustained; lyrical arc over motivic fragmentation
- **vs Bartok**: Denser melodic flow, warmer harmony; no cluster/sparse night-music
- **vs Hill**: Softer harmonic tension; suspended over chromatic

## When to Use in Hybrid Compositions

- **Lyrical lead** — Wheeler melody over Shorter or Bartok harmony
- **Chamber texture** — Wheeler sections between denser material
- **Suspended harmony** — Pair with Monk or Hill for contrast
- **Bridge material** — Wheeler for lift/contrast in AABA or song forms
