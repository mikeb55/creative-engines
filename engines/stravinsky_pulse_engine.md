# Stravinsky Pulse Engine

## Harmonic Language

Modal, quartal, polychord colours; occasional sharp dissonance. Use interval cycles, polychords, triad pairs. Avoid romantic harmonic language.

## Rhythmic Philosophy

Repeating rhythmic cells; irregular phrase lengths; syncopated ensemble hits; rhythmic dialogue. Shifting meters (4/4, 5/4, 7/8). Cells develop and recombine.

## Orchestration Behavior

*Adapt to any ensemble:* Primary (rhythmic motif leader); secondary (counter-rhythm); inner (rhythmic motor); bass (percussive anchor); harmonic colour (dyads, percussive attacks). Every voice contributes to rhythmic texture.

## Motif Development Approach

Rhythmic cells developed through displacement, orchestration change, recombination. No cell > 4 bars × 2 without transformation. Ensemble hits coordinated.

## Structural Tendencies

Intro (rhythmic cell) — A (development) — B (contrast) — A' (recombined cells) — Coda (rhythmic dissolution). Density arc: low → development → peak → release.
