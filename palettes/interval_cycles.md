# Interval Cycles

**Version 1.0**  
**Harmonic palette for composition engines**

---

## Purpose

Interval cycles provide systematic pitch organisation for harmonic depth. Use for Slonimsky-derived material, exotic harmony, or structural pitch design. Satisfies Rule 6 (Harmonic Depth).

---

## Cycle Types

### Cycle of Fifths

- C–G–D–A–E–B–F#–C#–G#–D#–A#–F–C
- Functional or non-functional application

### Cycle of Fourths

- C–F–Bb–Eb–Ab–Db–Gb–B–E–A–D–G–C
- Jazz-oriented; modal interchange

### Cycle of Minor Thirds

- C–Eb–F#–A–C (diminished)
- Symmetric; 3-fold division of octave

### Cycle of Major Thirds

- C–E–G#–C (augmented)
- Symmetric; 4-fold division of octave

### Cycle of Whole Tones

- C–D–E–F#–G#–A#–C
- 6-note symmetric scale

### Slonimsky Intervallic Cycles

- Systematic interval patterns (e.g. +1, +2, +3, -1, -2)
- Exotic melodic and harmonic movement

---

## Application

- Use for bass movement, chord roots, or melodic intervals
- Combine with triad pairs or polychords
- Avoid mechanical repetition; vary rhythm and orchestration
