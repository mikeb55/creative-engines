# Polychords

**Version 1.0**  
**Harmonic palette for composition engines**

---

## Purpose

Polychords combine two or more chord structures for harmonic depth and colour. Use for Stravinsky, Zappa, Slonimsky, or modern jazz vocabulary. Satisfies Rule 6 (Harmonic Depth).

---

## Notation

- **Slash notation:** Upper chord / Lower chord (e.g. D/C = D triad over C)
- **Stack notation:** Two triads or structures combined

---

## Common Polychord Types

### Triad over Triad

- C/Eb (C major over Eb major) — dark, minor-major
- D/F# (D over F#) — bright, Lydian
- Eb/C (Eb over C) — polychord colour

### Triad over Bass Note

- D/C, Em/C, F#/C — upper structure over pedal

### Seventh Chord over Triad

- Cmaj7/E — upper structure
- Am7/D — sus colour

### Quartal over Triad

- Quartal voicing over bass
- Example: So What voicing over root

### Collision Polychords (Zappa)

- Sharp dissonance
- Two unrelated structures
- Use sparingly; for disruption

---

## Application

- Use for chord symbols in score
- Voice-lead upper and lower structures independently
- Avoid random stacking; prefer voice-led logic
- For Zappa: collision permitted in disruption sections
